Gestalt Psychology

Principles of Gestalt Psychology
Principles of Grouping Definitions Images of Grouping
Similarity The principle of similarity is that elements that are similar to each other have a tendency to be perceived as a figure.
Proximity The principle of proximity explains that dots or lines which appear near one another have a tendency to group together to a figure.
Encloseness A figure will have well defined borders. If A, B, C, D are given and AB/CD constitute two self-enclosed units, then this arrangement rather than AC/ BD will be apprehended.
continuity Dots that are arranged on a plane or in a continuous row have dominance over other dots, even if they are nearby.
Figure and Ground If a person‘s entire field of vision has one single color, devoid of any type of distinction, then that person sees nothing. Therefore, the distinction between figure and background is an essential condition for perception, although only the emphasized figure becomes the focus of perception.
Simplicity The figure has a tendency to be perceived in as simple a form as possible, presenting a unified and well organized structure.
